Serious misconduct is unacceptable behaviour that:

  • falls significantly short of the standards expected of a teacher
  • brings the teaching profession into disrepute
  • affects the way someone performs their teaching role

If the person is convicted of a relevant offence at any time, this is also serious misconduct.

{% set examples %}

Examples might include:

  • committing a criminal offence that results in imprisonment (including a suspended sentence)
  • violence
  • terrorism
  • inappropriate sexual activity
  • voyeurism (including upskirting)
  • revenge pornography (sharing private, sexual materials, either photos or videos, of another person without their consent)
  • sexual communication with a child
  • viewing, taking, making, possessing, distributing or publishing any indecent photograph or image of a child (or permitting any such activity)
  • child cruelty and/or neglect
  • controlling or coercive behaviour
  • harassing and/or stalking
  • intolerance and/or hatred on the grounds of race, religion, sexual orientation or any of the other protected characteristics
  • possessing, supplying or producing class A, B or C drugs
  • fraud or serious dishonesty
  • theft from a person or other serious theft
  • arson and other major criminal damage
  • possession of prohibited firearms, knives or other weapons
  • serious offences where alcohol or drugs are involved
